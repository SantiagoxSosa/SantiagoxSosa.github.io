About me

In June 2026, I will be completing my Bachelor's/Master's degree in Electrical Engineering (ECE) at Drexel University as part of the BS/MS program. My academic focus is on advancing my knowledge in areas such as optics, robotics, and embedded systems, and I am eager to soon pursue a PhD in advanced optics & nanophotonics.

Currently, I am engaged in on-campus research, where I am contributing to projects that explore the integration of optics and signal processing for sensing applications by use of holographic films. This complements my prior experience at Lockheed Martin, where I worked on cutting-edge engineering solutions, gaining hands-on expertise in customer-facing research and development.

In the future, I aim to deepen my understanding of electro-optics and nanophotonics, with the goal of innovating within the realm of intelligent robotics and optical sensor design. My ambition is to develop groundbreaking technologies that merge these fields to solve real-world challenges in industries such as healthcare, defense, and environmental monitoring.

SIFT: Sugar Insight through Filter Technology

Non-invasive optical glucose monitoring using HPDLC technology, targeted toward continuous glucose monitoring innovations. This project combines expertise in optics and biomedical applications to create accessible health monitoring solutions.

The system leverages advanced holographic polymer-dispersed liquid crystals to create a breakthrough in non-invasive medical sensing, potentially revolutionizing diabetes management worldwide.
